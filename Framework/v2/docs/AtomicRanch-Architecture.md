# Atomic Ranch House - Architectural Description

## Style Overview

**Era:** Early 1960s California Suburban
**Style:** Atomic Ranch / Mid-Century Modern
**Influences:** Cliff May ranch houses, Eichler homes, Palm Springs modernism

## Key Characteristics

- Single-story horizontal profile
- Low-pitched roof (4:12 slope)
- Post-and-beam construction
- Large picture windows and glass walls
- Open floor plan with flowing spaces
- Indoor/outdoor living integration
- Carport (not enclosed garage)
- Front courtyard with privacy fence
- Clean lines, minimal ornamentation

---

## Dimensions

**Overall Footprint:** 72 ft wide x 48 ft deep (3,456 sq ft footprint)
**Living Area:** ~1,850 sq ft
**Carport:** 20 ft x 22 ft (440 sq ft)
**Front Courtyard:** 16 ft x 20 ft (320 sq ft)
**Rear Patio:** 24 ft x 12 ft (288 sq ft)

**Ceiling Height:** 8 ft standard, 10 ft in living room (cathedral)
**Wall Thickness:** 6 inches (exterior), 4 inches (interior)
**Foundation:** Concrete slab, 4 inches thick, extends 1 ft beyond walls

**Roof:**
- Pitch: 4:12 (18.4 degrees)
- Overhang: 2 ft on all sides
- Peak runs east-west (front to back)
- Low point at north and south edges

## VIEW: TOP (Plan View)

Looking down from above:

**Roof Shape:** Single gable running east-west
- Ridge line runs through center of house (front to back)
- North slope descends toward street
- South slope descends toward backyard
- 2 ft overhang on all sides creates shadow lines
- Flat section over carport (separate plane)

**Visible Elements:**
- Main roof rectangle: 76 ft x 52 ft (with overhangs)
- Carport roof: 24 ft x 26 ft (with overhangs)
- Gap between carport roof and main roof: courtyard visible
- Chimney: 3 ft x 2 ft, offset west of center in living room area
- Roof material: Built-up tar and gravel (flat gray appearance)

---

## VIEW: FRONT (North Elevation)

**Street Presence:** Low, horizontal, private

From left to right:
1. **Carport (West end)**
   - Open structure, post-and-beam
   - 2 posts supporting flat roof
   - 8 ft clearance
   - Concrete pad visible

2. **Privacy Fence**
   - 6 ft tall vertical wood slat fence
   - Connects carport to house
   - Obscures courtyard from street

3. **Courtyard (behind fence)**
   - Not visible from street
   - Entry door recessed within

4. **Main House Mass**
   - Low-slope roof descending toward viewer
   - Roof overhang creates deep shadow
   - Minimal windows on street side (privacy)
   - One horizontal clerestory window strip (2 ft tall x 12 ft wide)
   - Vertical wood siding or stucco
   - House number mounted on wall

5. **East End**
   - Solid wall (bedrooms behind)
   - One small bathroom window (frosted)

**Height Profile:**
- Carport: 8 ft flat
- Main roof: 8 ft at eave, rises to ~12 ft at ridge
- Overall horizontal emphasis

## Construction Sequence for Geometry

1. **Foundation Pad**
   - Main slab: 72 x 48 ft
   - Carport slab: 20 x 22 ft (connected)
   - Patio slab: 24 x 12 ft (rear extension)

2. **Exterior Walls** (solid, full height)
   - Perimeter walls as single masses
   - Include wall thickness (6 in)

3. **Interior Walls**
   - Room dividers (4 in thick)
   - Stop at ceiling height

4. **Ceiling Plane**
   - Flat at 8 ft for most areas
   - Vaulted section for living room (follows roof)

5. **Roof**
   - Main gable: ridge at ~12 ft, eaves at 8 ft
   - Carport: flat at 8 ft
   - Include overhangs

6. **CSG Subtractions**
   - Door openings (3 ft x 7 ft standard, 8 ft x 7 ft sliding)
   - Window openings (various sizes per elevation)
   - Clerestory strip

7. **Styling**
   - Apply materials per palette
   - Add accent elements (chimney, posts, fence)
